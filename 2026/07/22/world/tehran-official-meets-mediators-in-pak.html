Tehran official meets mediators in Pak. as Iran, U.S. trade strikes

BY ASSOCIATED PRESS

ISLAMABAD

Associated Press  |  Geopolitics  |  West Asia Conflicts

An Iranian official began meetings on Tuesday with mediators in Pakistan as diplomats sought to salvage the collapsed interim deal between Iran and the United States, even as both countries continued to launch attacks for a 10th day of renewed fighting.

The visit by Iran's Interior Minister Eskandar Momeni comes as Pakistan has intensified diplomatic efforts in recent days to resuscitate the deal.

Iran attacked a tanker in the Strait of Hormuz early Tuesday, forcing its crew to abandon the ship, while the United States conducted yet another round of air strikes targeting the Islamic Republic.

Tehran also hit U.S.-allied countries throughout the West Asia. Jordan military's said on Tuesday that Iran targeted it with five drones and three missiles, all of which were shot down. Bahrain sounded its missile alert sirens on Tuesday afternoon. In Kuwait, desalination and power plants were struck for a fourth straight day on Monday night.
