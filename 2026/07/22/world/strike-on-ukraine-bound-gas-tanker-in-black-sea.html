Strike on Ukraine-bound gas tanker in Black Sea wounds three seafarers

BY AGENCE FRANCE-PRESSE

BUCHAREST

Agence France-Presse  |  Geopolitics  |  Russia-Ukraine Conflict

A strike on a ship in the Black Sea carrying propane to the Ukrainian port of Reni wounded three seafarers and forced the crew's evacuation, Romania's President said on Tuesday.

Ukraine blamed Russia for the incident, describing it as a deliberate act of “terror”.

Russia made no immediate public comment.

“Last night, the LPG tanker Gas Lisbon, flying the Liberian flag, was struck outside Romanian territorial waters, approximately 20 nautical miles off the Romanian coast,” Romanian President Nicușor Dan said on X.

While authorities were still working to establish who had hit the tanker, the incident was “most likely part of the Russian Federation's illegal war of aggression against Ukraine,” Mr. Dan said.

The strike on the ship, which was carrying more than 3,790 tonnes of propane from the port of Alexandria in Egypt, caused a major fire on board the vessel.

After the strike, two Romanian rescue vessels evacuated the ship's 17 crew members, including three who “required emergency medical care”, the emergency services said.

Authorities did not immediately give details about the weapon involved in the strike.

“Two victims had burns covering approximately 10-15% of their bodies, and the third had suffered multiple wounds and was bleeding,” the statement said.

Ukraine's Foreign Minister Andriy Sybiga called the incident “yet another example of Russia's terror”.

“The response must match the scale of the threat. Stronger sanctions. Greater pressure on Russia,” he said.
