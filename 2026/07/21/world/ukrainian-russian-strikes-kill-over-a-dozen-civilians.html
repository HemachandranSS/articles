Ukrainian, Russian strikes kill over a dozen civilians

Agence France-Presse

Cross-border strikes intensify as Ukrainian drone attacks hit Belgorod and Moscow regions while Russian air bombardments target Zaporizhzhia, Pavlograd, Kramatorsk, and Odesa amid stalled peace talks.

International Relations  |  Global Security  |  Russia-Ukraine Conflict

A Ukrainian drone strike on a bus in Russia’s Belgorod region killed five people including a child on Monday, authorities said, while Kyiv said Russian strikes on several Ukrainian cities left at least eight dead.

Russia has escalated its bombardment of Ukraine, killing dozens of people in missile strikes since the beginning of the month, according to Ukrainian authorities. Kyiv has also intensified its retaliatory strikes.

In Russia’s Shebekino, a Ukrainian drone “deliberately and cynically struck a passenger coach”, the acting Governor of Russia’s Belgorod region said.

“Five civilians, including a minor, were killed as a result of this attack... In addition, 23 civilians were injured, including a teenager,” he added.

In Ukraine, Russian strikes on the cities of Pavlograd and Kramatorsk killed two people each, while Russian bombardment of the Ukrainian regions of Odesa and Zaporizhzhia killed four others, according to Ukrainian authorities.

Earlier on Monday, Ukraine launched scores of drones at the Moscow region, sparking fires and wounding at least 10 people, Russia said.

Both Russia and Ukraine – which deny targeting civilians – have launched repeated strikes on each other in recent months, as U.S.-led talks on ending the nearly four-and-a-half year conflict remain stalled.
