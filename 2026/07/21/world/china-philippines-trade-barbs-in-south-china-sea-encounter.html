China, Philippines trade barbs in South China Sea encounter

Associated Press

Manila accuses China’s coast guard of injuring a Filipino sailor at Second Thomas Shoal, while Beijing counters that Philippine boats rammed its vessel and attacked law enforcement personnel using oars and sticks.

International Relations  |  Maritime Security  |  South China Sea

The Philippine military on Monday accused China’s coast guard of allegedly injuring a Filipino sailor during an incident in the disputed Second Thomas Shoal in the South China Sea.

The military said Chinese coast guard personnel aboard a motorboat repeatedly struck the sailor on the head with a wooden baton, injuring him and damaging his smaller vessel.

China’s coast guard responded by claiming that two rubber boats belonging to a Filipino vessel had rammed the Chinese patrol boat. “During the incident, Philippine personnel attacked Chinese law enforcement personnel using oars and long sticks,” the Chinese coast guard said in a statement on social media.

The row was just the latest altercation between Chinese and Philippines personnel in disputed waters in the South China Sea, which Beijing claims virtually in its entirety.
