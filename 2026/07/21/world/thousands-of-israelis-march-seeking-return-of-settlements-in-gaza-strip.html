Thousands of Israelis march seeking return of settlements in Gaza Strip

Agence France-Presse

Demonstrators including settlers and right-wing activists gather near the border calling on the government to re-establish Israeli communities in the war-shattered Palestinian territory.

International Relations  |  West Asia Conflict  |  Geopolitics

Thousands of Israelis, including settlers and right-wing activists, marched towards the border with Gaza on Sunday, calling on the government to re-establish Israeli settlements in the war-shattered Palestinian territory.

The demonstrators gathered near the border before walking in large numbers towards the fence overlooking northern Gaza, where Israeli security forces were deployed to prevent participants from advancing further.

“We are walking today to tell the government of Israel and show all the world that this land belongs to us and that Gaza really belongs to us and that we want to go back there,” a participant said.

Another participant said the event marked what he viewed as “just the beginning” of a campaign to restore Israeli communities in the coastal territory.

“We are having a big march back to the Gaza Strip, back to where Jews lived for hundreds, thousands of years,” he said, adding that the 2005 withdrawal had been temporary.

Previous marches have likewise sought to pressure the government of Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu to re-establish settlements in the territory—much of which has been rendered uninhabitable by more than two years of war.
