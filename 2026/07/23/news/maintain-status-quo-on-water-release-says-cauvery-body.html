Maintain status quo on water release, says Cauvery body

CWMA directs Tamil Nadu and Karnataka to use present storage only for drinking purposes; it takes note of poor rainfall in basin, says Karnataka released only 0.54 tmcft of water in July so far

B.S. SATISH KUMAR — BENGALURU

Polity & Governance  |  Inter-State River Disputes, Water Resource Management & Federal Institutional Oversight

Taking a serious note of poor rainfall in the Cauvery basin, the Cauvery Water Management Authority (CWMA), which met in Delhi on Wednesday, has directed both Karnataka and Tamil Nadu to strictly confine use of their storage to drinking water purposes.

The CWMA, which is the apex body that monitors implementation of Cauvery water release to lower riparian States as per the Supreme Court’s verdict, directed both the States to maintain status quo with respect to water release and utilisation.

According to sources, the CWMA referred to the IMD forecast that indicates further tough time in terms of rainfall in the coming days, and observed that it is going to be a distress year for the Cauvery basin.

The present storage in reservoirs of the Cauvery basin in both Karnataka and Tamil Nadu could only take care of their drinking water requirements, the authority reportedly observed.

Warning both the States against using the water storage for other purposes including irrigation, the authority expressed confidence that it would be possible to prevent any drinking water shortage if the storage in reservoirs is judiciously used, sources said.

At the meeting, it was also noted that Karnataka had released only 2.5 tmcft of water in June and 0.54 tmcft during July so far to Tamil Nadu, which is lower than that prescribed.

Review on July 28
The situation will be reviewed on July 28 by the Cauvery Water Regulation Committee, which assists the CWMA in monitoring the storage, releases and other issues.
