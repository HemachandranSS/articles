Govt. ready for discussion on NEET paper leak: Rijiju

Minister says Speaker will hold consultations and decide rules, date, duration of debate; Congress leader seeks adjournment motion to set aside all other business; demands Pradhan's resignation

THE HINDU BUREAU — NEW DELHI

Polity & Governance  |  Parliamentary Devices & Public Administration

Amid repeated disruptions, Parliamentary Affairs Minister Kiren Rijiju on Wednesday told the Lok Sabha that the government was ready for a discussion on the NEET paper leak, even as Opposition parties insisted that Union Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan resign over the controversy.

The Opposition protests led to repeated adjournments of the House as soon as the proceedings started at 11 a.m. When the Lok Sabha reassembled at noon, Speaker Om Birla asked Mr. Rijiju to make a statement.

Congress leader K.C. Venugopal intervened, saying the Opposition's demand went beyond a discussion and included Mr. Pradhan's resignation.

“Our demand is simple. When the entire nation is feeling the pain of our students, all other business of the House should be set aside and this burning issue should be the top priority for Parliament,” Mr. Venugopal said.

He demanded that Mr. Birla allow an adjournment motion — a parliamentary device where the House adjourns all other business and starts debating the subject for which the motion has been moved.

Responding to him, Mr. Rijiju said the government was ready for a discussion on the issue. He said the Speaker would hold consultations with floor leaders to decide the rules, date, and duration of the debate.

Disruptions and protest
Mr. Birla then said he had heard the views of all sides, as well as the government's willingness to discuss the matter “with an open mind”.

He urged the Opposition members to participate in the debate instead of entering the Well of the House and disrupting proceedings.

Amid slogan shouting by the Opposition members, the House was again adjourned till 2 p.m.

The Congress and Samajwadi Party MPs were dressed in black to protest against the alleged paper leak and the police action against demonstrators at Jantar Mantar and elsewhere.

Later, speaking to reporters outside Parliament, Mr. Rijiju said the government was prepared for a “detailed discussion” so that the country could know the steps taken to address the controversy.

The Union Minister accused the Opposition of preferring street protests over a parliamentary debate, saying that issues affecting students and the youth should be discussed in Parliament.

“Street protests will not yield results,” Mr. Rijiju said.

Separately, Mr. Pradhan met the Speaker in Parliament House.

The Opposition has been pressing for a discussion on alleged examination irregularities while insisting on Mr. Pradhan’s resignation over his handling of the issue.
