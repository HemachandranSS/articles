Protesting students are not terrorists, Modi must apologise to them: Rahul

Opposition in '100% agreement' with the protesters' demands, including Pradhan's resignation, he says

SANDEEP PHUKAN — NEW DELHI

Polity & Governance  |  Executive Accountability & Educational Governance

Prime Minister Narendra Modi should apologise, Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan must resign and everyone who assaulted the students must be held accountable, Leader of the Opposition in the Lok Sabha Rahul Gandhi said on Wednesday, a day after his dramatic protest outside the Prime Minister’s residence.

“They [the students] are our future. They are not terrorists,” Mr. Gandhi said at a press conference.

Claiming that there had been 152 examination paper leaks over the past decade, he alleged that about 7.5 crore students had been affected while “there have been zero convictions”. “Somebody or a group of people are destroying our education system, India’s biggest asset,” he said.

‘Legitimate demands’
Mr. Gandhi said the Opposition was “100% in agreement” with the protesters’ demands, including Mr. Pradhan’s resignation, and described them as “legitimate” and “non-negotiable”.

At the start of the press conference, the Congress leader played videos showing police action against students during Monday’s “Sansad Chalo” protest.

Referring to the visuals, Mr. Gandhi questioned why students demanding a fair education system were subjected to force. “What have they done to have security forces aiming weapons at them, to be beaten up, to be thrashed? They are protesting peacefully, asking for what this country owes them,” he said.

Mr. Gandhi reiterated the Congress’s charge that the country’s education system had been systematically undermined under the Narendra Modi government. “Our education system, which was known to be one of the best in the world, is now a rigged system,” he said.

The Congress leader also accused the government of imposing a heavy financial burden on families through an expensive coaching ecosystem. Citing party estimates, he claimed that Indian families spend about ₹1.32 lakh crore annually on examinations and coaching, nearly matching the Union government’s education budget of ₹1.4 lakh crore, only to be told later that examination papers had been leaked.

Calling Mr. Pradhan “incapable” of handling the education portfolio, Mr. Gandhi said the Education Minister had “failed” and should be relieved of his responsibilities.

Asked about his detention and the alleged manhandling by police, Mr. Gandhi brushed aside the issue. “Yes, I was dragged. But, the policemen whispered into my ears to please remove this government,” he said.
