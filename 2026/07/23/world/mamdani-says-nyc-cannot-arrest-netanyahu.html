Mamdani says NYC cannot arrest Netanyahu, urges federal govt. to act

New York Mayor Zohran Mamdani acknowledges municipal limitations in executing ICC warrants but calls on the U.S. federal government to arrest the Israeli Prime Minister during the UN General Assembly

ASSOCIATED PRESS — NEW YORK

International Relations & Security  |  International Criminal Law, Jurisdictional Enforcement & Sovereign Immunity

New York Mayor Zohran Mamdani said late Tuesday that the city would not be able to execute an arrest warrant issued by the world’s top war-crimes court for Israel’s Benjamin Netanyahu, but urged the federal government to do so.

In the video, Mr. Mamdani described the Israeli Prime Minister as a “war criminal” and said he is not welcome in New York City.

“It is clear that we do not have the independent legal authority to enforce this warrant,” he said in a video posted on X. “The federal government however does and I call on them to join the ICC and execute this warrant,” he added.

Mr. Mamdani had told the New York Times earlier this month that he and the city’s legal department were discussing arresting Mr. Netanyahu if he comes to New York City for the UN General Assembly in September.

President Donald Trump on Monday told Mr. Netanyahu in a social media post that he “will not be arrested, in any way, shape, or form, while in the United States of America.”

Mr. Mamdani had said throughout his mayoral election campaign last year that he would seek to order the city’s police to arrest Mr. Netanyahu under the criminal court’s warrant over Mr. Netanyahu’s role in the war in Gaza.

In his video message on Tuesday, Mr. Mamdani said his administration has reviewed all avenues available and determined the city can’t execute the arrest warrant.

Danny Danon, the Israeli ambassador to the UN, responded by telling the mayor “enough”.

“You were elected to serve New Yorkers, not Hamas’ propaganda,” he said.
