U.S. to announce nuclear agreement with Saudi Arabia

The landmark 30-year agreement will establish a civilian nuclear programme in the kingdom, raising non-proliferation concerns among U.S. lawmakers and Israeli officials

AGENCE FRANCE-PRESSE — WASHINGTON

International Relations & Security  |  Civilian Nuclear Cooperation, Non-Proliferation & Middle East Geopolitics

The United States plans to announce a landmark deal with Saudi Arabia on Wednesday that would help establish a civilian nuclear programme in the kingdom, according to media reports.

The move comes as Washington fights a war with Iran that began in part over U.S. concerns with Tehran’s own nuclear program — an issue that remains a key point of contention during now-stalled talks between the two foes.

The New York Times, citing two unidentified U.S. officials, reported on Tuesday that the Trump administration planned to formally sign and announce the deal with the Saudis on Wednesday.

President Donald Trump’s administration said the agreement would provide billions of dollars for the U.S. nuclear industry.

A provision in the 30-year deal would potentially see U.S. companies build a uranium enrichment facility in Saudi Arabia, The Wall Street Journal reported.

However, U.S. lawmakers from both parties and Israeli officials have voiced opposition to a civilian nuclear project for Saudi Arabia over fears it could be converted to eventually develop nuclear weapons.
