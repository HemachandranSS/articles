Will not tolerate corruption, CM warns Ministers, officials

The Hindu Bureau

Chairing a Cabinet meeting, Vijay makes it clear that he will not hesitate to take action. He also asks Ministers not to make a spectacle out of inspections related to their departments

News  |  Governance  |  Administration

Chief Minister C. Joseph Vijay on Thursday warned his Cabinet colleagues and senior officials that he would not tolerate corruption or irregularities in their departments. Sources said the Chief Minister made it clear during a Cabinet meeting that he would not hesitate to take action against anyone found guilty, and that no one could fault him for doing so.

“Do not think that we will be in power for only five years, and that you can do whatever you want. We will continue to govern the State,” he is learnt to have said at the meeting. Mr. Vijay also advised the Ministers not to make a spectacle out of inspections related to their departments.

“We are here to serve the people. Do not bring the government into disrepute. I will also take action against officials who are unwilling to cooperate with the government in implementing its programmes and achieving its goals,” he said.

Budget discussion

Details of the upcoming State Budget were also discussed during the Cabinet meeting. Speaking to reporters after the meeting, Law Minister R. Nirmalkumar said it was a routine meeting ahead of the presentation of the Budget. “In today’s Cabinet meeting, deliberations were held on what should be included in the Budget, and how it should be presented. These matters cannot be disclosed publicly yet,” the Minister said.

Custodial death

To a query, he said strict action would be taken against those responsible for the death of remand prisoner S. Sabari Varman in Nagercoil.

“If a death occurs in prison, this government will definitely take stringent action. Previous governments used to cover up such incidents. However, based on the Chief Minister’s orders, cases were registered two days ago, and everyone involved has been arrested,” he said. He added that in cases of prison or lock-up deaths, the government would stand by the victims.

DMK-AIADMK-BJP ‘pact’

Claiming that the DMK, the AIADMK, and the BJP were trying to form a government together, he sarcastically said, “Unable to run their own parties, the DMK and the AIADMK might merge with the BJP in two or three years, securing, perhaps, two Cabinet Minister posts each for (DMK president) Mr. Stalin’s family and (AIADMK general secretary) Mr. Palaniswami’s family.”

The Minister claimed that attempts were being made to “buy” TVK MLAs, with offers ranging from ₹30 crore to ₹50 crore.

Alleging corruption in the Registration Department over the past five years, the Minister levelled serious charges against former Registration Minister P. Moorthy. “A person was stationed at every registration office to collect the ‘Minister’s cutting’ (commission). If you were to register a property worth ₹1 lakh, ₹20,000 would be spent on bribes,” the Minister claimed.

He emphasised that if anyone demands a bribe, action will be taken immediately if a complaint is lodged via the toll-free number.

When asked about the cancellation of several projects initiated during the previous administration, he said, “Projects in every department were designed with a 20% to 30% commission built into them. By revising old tenders, we are cutting down the money meant for commission. We have instructed officials to eliminate the 30% commission that was taken previously, and resubmit the tenders.”

Regarding the appointment of government lawyers, he said that 80% to 90% of the appointments had already been made. If any unsuitable or inept individuals were found to have been appointed, they will be replaced, he added.
