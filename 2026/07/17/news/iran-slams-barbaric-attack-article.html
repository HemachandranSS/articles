Iran slams ‘barbaric’ U.S. attack near hospital

Agence France-Presse

Tehran says the strike forced evacuation of patients near a cancer hospital in southwest Iran

News  |  West Asia  |  Conflict

Tehran on Thursday accused the United States of carrying out a “barbaric attack” after a cancer hospital in Iran’s southwest, Ahvaz, was forced to evacuate and the patients transferred to other medical centres due to nearby strikes.

“This barbaric attack, reminiscent of Israel’s atrocities against health-care facilities, caused severe suffering and anxiety upon the hospitalised children,” Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baqaei posted on X, saying “211 patients undergoing chemotherapy” were evacuated.

“This barbaric attack... caused severe suffering and anxiety upon the hospitalised children.”

Hani, a 34-year-old teacher from Ahvaz, said the strikes were “very intense”, adding: “My hands are shaking. There were at least 11, 12 explosions. My ears are exploding.”
