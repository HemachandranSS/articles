Iran’s top negotiator defends US peace talks

Patrick Sykes & Golnar Motevalli

Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf and other hardliners push back as tensions rise after the ceasefire

News  |  West Asia  |  Diplomacy

Iran’s lead negotiator defended diplomatic efforts to end the war, pushing back against hardliners urging him to abandon a tentative peace deal and avenge the country’s slain supreme leader.

Negotiation is not tantamount to compromise and, alongside military strength, is part of the strategy of resistance and the safeguarding of national interests, Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf said in an interview on state TV late Wednesday.

We must be able to create a synergy between the military and diplomatic approaches, he said, adding that to isolate and select one of these two methods as the sole solution is a strategic error.

The comments appeared to address Iranian officials who’ve framed moderates like Ghalibaf and Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi as traitors for agreeing to a ceasefire in June that they see as a trap designed to buy time for the US to resume a war Iran was winning.

Ghalibaf didn’t mention anyone by name, citing instead questions being asked among the people and various groups, and urging unity among all Iranians.

“We must be able to create a synergy between the military and diplomatic approaches.”

His address reflects increasing tensions between Iran’s hawks and pragmatists. The latter group want to prioritise economic recovery and see diplomacy as one of the avenues to achieve it. The rift intensified amid the funeral for former Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, which coincided with the rekindling of attacks between the US and Iran.

The split also played out on the front pages of the nation’s newspapers this week. On Wednesday, the elected government’s official newspaper, Iran, highlighted comments by Ali Khamenei’s eldest son Mostafa that “patience is no way incompatible with taking revenge.”

The June ceasefire effectively collapsed after both sides resumed attacks, with the US reimposing a blockade on Iranian ports and Tehran declaring the Strait of Hormuz shut.
