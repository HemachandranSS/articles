10 Key Terms

NSE (National Stock Exchange of India)

Entity

India's largest stock exchange by trading volume, headquartered in Mumbai. It provides a platform for buying and selling financial instruments like equities, bonds, and derivatives. It operates electronic, screen-based trading and maintains market transparency.

Example Sentence

"The NSE processed over ₹80,000 crore worth of equity trades in a single session, making it one of the world's busiest exchanges."

01

SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)

Entity

The statutory regulatory body that oversees and governs the securities markets in India. SEBI protects investor interests, regulates stock exchanges, and approves financial products before they are launched to the public.

Example Sentence

"Before launching its new index fund, the asset management company had to obtain formal approval from SEBI."

02

Futures (Futures Contract)

Financial Instrument

A legal agreement to buy or sell a specific commodity or financial asset at a predetermined price at a specified future date. Futures are standardised, traded on an exchange, and used both for hedging risk and for speculative profit.

Example Sentence

"An airline bought crude oil futures six months in advance to lock in fuel prices and protect itself against a potential price spike."

03

Price Discovery

Concept

The process through which buyers and sellers in a market collectively determine the fair price of an asset or commodity. Transparent, competitive markets facilitate efficient price discovery, reflecting all available information about supply, demand, and sentiment.

Example Sentence

"The introduction of a commodity exchange improved price discovery for wheat, helping farmers know the actual market value of their harvest before selling."

04

Monthly Contracts

Market Term

Futures contracts that expire at the end of each calendar month. In this case, NSE will offer 12 simultaneously active monthly contracts, meaning traders can buy or sell gas futures up to one year in advance at any given time.

Example Sentence

"A gas distributor entered into monthly contracts for the next six months to ensure a stable supply price regardless of seasonal demand fluctuations."

05

IGX (Indian Gas Exchange)

Entity

India's first automated national-level gas trading platform, established to enable transparent spot-market trading of natural gas. IGX connects buyers and sellers of natural gas across different delivery points via an online exchange mechanism.

Example Sentence

"A fertiliser plant sourced its gas requirements for the upcoming week directly through IGX, benefiting from competitive spot prices offered by multiple suppliers."

06

Spot Trading

Concept

The purchase or sale of a commodity or financial instrument for immediate delivery and payment, as opposed to a future date. Spot prices reflect the current market value of an asset. IGX facilitates this kind of trading specifically for natural gas in India.

Example Sentence

"The power plant bought gas at the spot trading price when futures costs were too high, paying the current market rate for next-day delivery."

07

Derivatives

Financial Instrument

Financial contracts whose value is derived from the performance of an underlying asset, index, or rate — such as commodities, stocks, interest rates, or currencies. Futures, options, and swaps are all types of derivatives. They are used for risk management, speculation, and arbitrage.

Example Sentence

"The company used interest rate derivatives to hedge its exposure to rising borrowing costs on its long-term loans."

08

Hedging

Strategy

A risk management strategy in which an investor or business takes an offsetting position in a related asset to reduce the impact of adverse price movements. Hedging limits potential losses but may also cap potential gains. It is commonly used by producers and consumers of commodities.

Example Sentence

"The city gas distributor used natural gas futures as a hedging tool, locking in a supply price for the winter months and avoiding the risk of a sudden cost surge."

09

Price Volatility

Market Term

The degree to which the price of an asset fluctuates over a given period. High volatility means prices swing dramatically in short timeframes, increasing uncertainty and financial risk for buyers and sellers. Natural gas is known for its seasonal and geopolitical price volatility.

Example Sentence

"Extreme price volatility in natural gas markets during the winter of 2021 caused several power generators to face unexpected losses as costs tripled within weeks."
